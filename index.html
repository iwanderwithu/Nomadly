🇪🇸 Spain Foreign Buyer Rules (PR / US Citizens)
✅ You Can Buy If
• You have a valid NIE number
• You have a Spanish bank account
• You can show proof of funds for purchase
• No minimum residency required to buy
• Non-residents can get a mortgage (20–30% down)
💸 Buying Costs to Budget
• ITP tax (resale): 8–10% of price
• IVA (new build): 10% + 1.5% AJD
• Notary + registry: ~1–2%
• Gestor (agent) fees: ~1%
• Mortgage arrangement: ~0.5–1%
• Total buying cost: ~10–13% on top of price
🏙️ Best Areas for Appreciation
• Málaga — 8–12% YoY, coastal, expat demand
• Valencia — 6–9% YoY, undervalued vs Barcelona
• Madrid outskirts — new transport links boosting values
• Alicante / Costa Blanca — strong rental market
• Seville — lowest entry price, growing tourism
🏦 Non-Resident Mortgage
• Max loan: 70% of appraised value
• Min down: 30% + 10–13% buying costs
• Rate: ~3.2–4.5% variable / 3.5–5% fixed
• Best banks for non-residents: Sabadell, Bankinter, Santander
• NIE + Spanish bank account required

Spain Non-Lucrative Visa (NLV)
Official requirements · Updated 2026 · Source: exteriores.gob.es
💰
Min. Income
€2,259/mo
📅
Visa Valid
365 days
⏱️
Processing
~1 month
🏆
PR Citizenship
2 years
What is it?
A visa to reside in Spain without working. You must prove you have sufficient means of subsistence through passive income (investments, rental income, pensions, savings). This visa does not authorize gainful employment with Spanish companies but allows remote work for non-Spanish employers in a legal grey area. It is renewable and counts toward the 2-year citizenship fast track for Puerto Ricans and Latin Americans.
Who can apply?
✓ Applicant
Main applicant with sufficient income/savings
✓ Spouse/Partner
Registered partner or stable partner
✓ Minor Children
Unmarried minor children of applicant
✓ Adult Children
With disability or unable to provide for themselves
Required Documents
⚠️ All foreign documents must be legalized or apostilled, and translated to Spanish if not already in Spanish.
📝
National Visa Application Form
Completed and signed by each applicant. Minors signed by parent/guardian.
📋
EX-01 Form
Application form for non-lucrative residence authorization. Complete all sections.
📸
Passport Photo
Recent, passport-sized, color photo on light background. No glasses or face coverings.
🛂
Valid Passport
Original + photocopy of biometric data pages. Valid for at least 1 year, 2 blank pages. Max 10 years old.
💰
Proof of Financial Means
Documents proving min. €2,259/mo income OR equivalent savings. Add 25% per dependent family member.
🏥
Health Insurance
Certificate from insurer authorized in Spain. Must cover ALL risks of Spanish public health system. No copays.
🔍
Criminal Record Certificate
From all countries of residence in last 5 years. Apostilled + translated.
🩺
Medical Certificate
Certifying no diseases with serious public health risk per International Health Regulations 2005.
📍
Proof of Residence in Consular District
Document proving you reside in the consulate's jurisdiction.
💳
Payment of Fees
Form 790, code 052, section 2.1. Can be paid online.
Procedure & Timeline
1
Submit Application
In person at the consulate. Minors may use a legal representative. Bring ALL documents + copies.
2
Receive Receipt
Consulate gives you a receipt with a tracking code to check status online at sutramiteconsular.maec.es
3
Wait for Decision
Legal time limit is 1 month from the day after receiving favorable residence authorization.
4
Collect Visa
In person by applicant or legal representative. No appointment needed for collection.
5
Travel to Spain
Visa valid for 365 days. Enter Spain and apply for TIE (Foreigner Identity Card) within 30 days.
⚖️ If Refused
Refusal is always in writing with reasons. You may: (1) File an appeal for reconsideration within 1 month, or (2) File an administrative appeal with the High Court of Justice of Madrid within 2 months.
🇵🇷 Puerto Rico Fast Track Tip
As a Puerto Rican (Iberoamerican national), your NLV counts toward the 2-year citizenship fast track from day one. Puerto Rico has its own Consulado General de España at Edificio Mercantil Plaza, Piso 11, Av. Ponce de León s/n, Hato Rey PR 00918 — no need to travel to Miami. 📞 787-758-6090 · 📧 [email protected] · Hours: Mon–Fri 8:30am–1:30pm. Appointment required. Current wait times: 4–10 weeks. Documents in English need a sworn translator (traductor jurado) — budget $150–300.

Spain Digital Nomad Visa
Launched 2023 · Ley de Startups
💰
Min. Income
€2,646/mo
📅
Initial Visa
1 year
🔄
Renewal
2+3 years
💳
Tax Rate
24% flat
Designed for remote workers and freelancers employed by non-Spanish companies. Allows you to legally work in Spain for foreign clients. The Beckham Law (Ley Beckham) applies — a flat 24% income tax rate on the first €600,000 for up to 6 years instead of Spain's progressive rates (up to 47%).
Key Requirements
💼
Remote Employment
Must work for company(ies) headquartered outside Spain. Max 20% income from Spanish clients.
💰
Income Proof
Minimum €2,646/month (200% SMI). Employment contract or client contracts as freelancer.
🏥
Health Insurance
Private health insurance covering Spain with no copays.
🔍
Background Check
Clean criminal record from all countries of residence in last 5 years.
📄
Company Evidence
Proof company exists for 1+ year and you've worked there 3+ months (employees) or 3+ month client relationship (freelancers).
💡 NLV vs Digital Nomad Visa
If you have active remote income (salary, freelance), choose the Digital Nomad Visa — it's legal and gives the Beckham Law tax benefit. If you have passive income (investments, pension, rental), choose the NLV. Both count toward the 2-year citizenship track for Puerto Ricans.

Spain — Citizenship by Descent
Spanish parent or grandparent born in Spain
⏱️
Timeline
4–6 months
💰
Income Required
None
🛂
Result
Full EU Passport
Who Qualifies
You qualify if a parent or grandparent was born in Spain and held Spanish citizenship. The key requirement is that no one in the lineage renounced Spanish citizenship. Post-2025 changes have not affected this route — it remains fully open.
Two Pathways
🏛️ Apply in Spain
Requires 90-day stay. Apply at any Registro Civil. Faster: 4–6 months. Can choose any city — not restricted to ancestor's hometown.
🏢 Via Consulate
Apply at San Juan consulate — no need to travel. Longer wait: 1–3 years. No residency required.
Required Documents
📄 Your birth certificate (apostilled + translated)
📄 Parent/grandparent's birth certificate from Spanish Registro Civil
📄 Marriage certificates throughout the lineage
📄 Certificate of Non-Naturalization for Spanish ancestor
📄 Death certificates for deceased in chain (if applicable)
📄 Valid US passport + copies
📝 All documents must be apostilled and translated by a traductor jurado
🇵🇷 Puerto Rico Advantage
Puerto Ricans with Spanish ancestry have a direct path to Spanish citizenship without needing a long-term visa first. Once citizenship is granted, you have full EU freedom of movement — live, work, and travel in all 27 EU countries. Your US citizenship is not affected.

Spain — Entrepreneur / Startup Visa
Ley de Startups 2023 · Official requirements · Updated 2026
💰
Min. Funds
€600/mo or €31k savings
⚡
ENISA Decision
10–20 biz days
📅
Permit Duration
3 yrs → +2 yrs
💳
Corp. Tax (startup)
15% + 24% personal
Spain's Ley de Startups (Law 28/2022) is one of the most accessible entrepreneur visa programs in the EU. No minimum capital investment is required — your business plan is evaluated by ENISA for innovative character and economic impact (10–20 business days). Residence permit then processed by UGE in ~20 business days. The initial permit is 3 years (renewable for 2 more). Spain's Golden Visa ended April 2025, making this a top innovation immigration route.
Key Requirements
💡
Innovative Business Plan → ENISA Evaluation
Tech, ICT, green energy, health sciences, biotech, aerospace. Must show scalability beyond local market, market need, and economic value for Spain. Company must be under 5 years old (7 yrs for biotech/energy). No traditional brick-and-mortar businesses — must be tech-enabled or high-growth.
💰
Financial Means (one-time bank statement proof)
Main applicant: €600/mo (100% IPREM) or ~€31,752 in savings. Spouse: +€300/mo. Per child: +€150/mo. This is a savings proof — NOT a monthly income requirement.
🏥
Health Insurance
Public or private insurance authorized to operate in Spain. Cigna Global, Aetna International, Allianz Care are popular options (~$89–250/mo).
🔍
Criminal Background Check
FBI check (apostilled at US Dept of State) + PR local record (apostilled at PR Dept of State). No serious criminal history in last 5 years. Must not have been a Spanish tax resident in the previous 5 years.
💳 Tax Benefits (Ley de Startups)
Corporate Tax — 15%First 4 profitable fiscal years (vs. 25% standard). Tax deferral available in first 2 profitable years with no interest or guarantees.
Beckham Law — 24% PersonalFlat rate on income up to €600k for 6 years. Requires no Spanish tax residency in previous 5 years. Also applies to co-founders and employees.
Stock Options€50k/year tax-free (raised from €12k). Tax on amounts above €50k deferred until liquidity event (IPO, sale, etc.).
Angel Investor Deduction50% deduction on investments in new companies (raised from 30%). Annual cap: €100k. Window: first 5 years of company (7 for biotech/energy).
🚀 Entrepreneur vs Digital Nomad Visa
Entrepreneur VisaBuilding a startup in Spain · ~€31k savings proof · ENISA business plan required · 15% corp tax + 24% Beckham · 3yr permit → 2yr renewal · 5yr → permanent residency
Digital Nomad VisaWorking remotely for foreign employers · Min €2,646/mo income · No business plan · Simpler process · 1yr visa → 3yr permit → 5yr permanent · 24% Beckham
🇵🇷 Puerto Rico Advantage
PR founders benefit from the 2-year citizenship fast track as Iberoamericanos. Spain's Ley de Startups is designed to attract global talent — your PR background, bilingual skills, and US business connections are real advantages. Many PR founders choose Madrid or Barcelona for access to European venture capital. Málaga's emerging tech scene (Málaga TechPark) is an affordable alternative.

⚖️ Spanish Rental Law — What You Need to Know
Lease Length
Min 5 years tenant protection (LAU 2019). Landlord can't kick you out before 5 years without cause.
Deposit
1 month unfurnished · 2 months furnished. Landlord must hold in regulated account.
Agency Fees
Since 2023: landlord pays the agent — not you. If an agent charges you, it's illegal.
Empadronamiento
Landlord must sign your empadronamiento (city registration). Confirm BEFORE signing lease.
Utilities
Usually NOT included. Budget €100–150/mo for electricity, water, internet. Verify with landlord.
PR to Spain Tips
Most landlords want: passport, bank statements (3 months), and proof of income/savings for NLV. Spotahome lets you book without a NIE.
